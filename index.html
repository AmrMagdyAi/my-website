The diagnosis

Why real estate CRMs quietly fail

Most property companies don't have a software problem — they have an architecture and adoption problem. Here's what I find inside the orgs I audit.

The system gets built. Nobody uses it.

Agents revert to spreadsheets and texts, leads go cold, and leadership flies blind. The fix isn't more features — it's the right architecture plus training that makes the CRM the easiest path, not the hardest.

Brittle Zapier sprawl

Dozens of stacked zaps silently break, drop leads, and burn through task limits with no audit trail.

Manual call logging

Reps log calls by hand — or not at all — so activity data is incomplete and follow-up slips through.

Problem

30+ brittle Zapier automations, calls logged by hand, zero pipeline visibility for leadership, and low Salesforce adoption across the team.

Approach

Built a centralized Configuration Object (50+ rules, MergeFieldReplacer Apex) and migrated 30+ Zapier automations to native Flows. Outcome

+30% pipeline visibility, 20+ hours per week given back to the team, and +25% Salesforce user adoption — with manual call logging eliminated entirely.
